Hi, I'm Jaweria!

(pronounced Ja-where-ya)

I’m a social psychologist and science communicator passionate about exploring how emotions connect us. I'm currently a PhD candidate at the University of Toronto, (supervisor: Dr. Jennifer Stellar. Beyond my research, I enjoy teaching, mentoring, and turning science into art through writing, public talks, and creative projects. In all my work, I aim to make research accessible, engaging, and human-centered, inviting others to see research as something to learn from, feel, and share.

I investigate emotions, prosociality, & morality.

I take a basic science approach to understand how we experience emotions, how we perceive them in others, and how they shape our relationships. Using psychophysiological methods and advanced data analytics, I study these complex, human constructs and transform findings into clear, compelling narratives. My work has been published in Affective Science, recognized nationally by SSHRC, and shared across conferences, workshops, and creative media.

Compassion Experience

How does compassion shape us, both psychologically and physiologically? I study how compassion changes with repeated exposure to others’ suffering and across emotional contexts.

Compassion for Others

How does compassion change in response to others’ emotions? My work explores how our physiology attunes to others and whether empathy helps us accurately perceive group emotions.

Empathy & Morality

How does empathy guide moral life? I investigate how people navigate moral disagreements and whether empathy can bridge divides to drive prosocial behavior.




I teach to engage minds within and beyond the classroom.

I create inclusive, hands-on learning experiences in psychology, psychophysiology, and data science. My experience includes teaching undergraduate guest lectures and provincial, national, and international workshops on psychology, psychophysiology, and data science for both high school and university students.

I'm passionate about turning research into art.

Through visual storytelling and multimedia projects, I explore new ways of making psychological science feel personal and digestable. My creative work bridges the gap between data and lived experience, showing that science isn’t just something to read—it’s something to see, feel, and share.
