Hi, I'm Jaweria!

(pronounced Ja-where-ya)

I’m a social psychologist and science communicator passionate about exploring how emotions connect us. I'm currently a PhD candidate at the University of Toronto, (supervisor: Dr. Jennifer Stellar. Beyond my research, I enjoy teaching, mentoring, and turning science into art through writing, public talks, and creative projects. In all my work, I aim to make research accessible, engaging, and human-centered, inviting others to see research as something to learn from, feel, and share.

I investigate emotions, prosociality, & morality.

I take a basic science approach to understanding how we experience emotions, how we perceive them in others, and how they shape our relationships. Using psychophysiological methods and advanced data analytics, I study these complex human processes and translate findings into clear, compelling narratives. My work has been published in Affective Science, recognized nationally by SSHRC, and shared across conferences, workshops, and creative media.

My research centers on three interconnected questions:

  • COMPASSION & EXPERIENCE
    How does compassion unfold psychologically and physiologically, and how does it change with repeated exposure to others’ suffering across emotional contexts?
  • PERCEIVING OTHERS’ EMOTIONS
    How do people perceive emotions in others—particularly in social and group contexts—and when does physiological attunement support or constrain empathic accuracy?
  • EMPATHY, MORALITY, & DISAGREEMENT
    How does empathy shape moral judgment, disagreement, and prosocial behavior, and can it bridge divides between people with differing values?
